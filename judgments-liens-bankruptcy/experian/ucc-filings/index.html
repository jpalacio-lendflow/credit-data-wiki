UCC Filings

Companies that extend secured loans to other businesses file Uniform Commercial Code (UCC) documents with the appropriate secretary of state office to protect their interest in the asset. UCC filings may include one of the following types of collateral which have been pledged:

  • Accounts
  • Accounts receivable
  • Contracts
  • Hereafter acquired property
  • Inventory
  • Leases
  • Notes receivable
  • Proceeds

Legal Type​

The type of action the creditor is filing

Legal Action​

Type of legal action assigned to the Assignee

Assignee​

The entity the legal filing is assigned to

Definition​

Defined or Undefined

Document Number​

The specific number or identifier that is associated with the file that was submitted to the Secretary of State

Collateral Code​

A code or description of the type of collateral defined in the system

Secured Party​

The secured party is the lender, seller or other entity that has rights to the collateral pledged against a loan in the event that the debtor defaults.
